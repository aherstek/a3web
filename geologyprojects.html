Geological projects

Mapping

At the intersection of art and science lies the expertise of MapGeo specialists. With a meticulous approach to cartography, they craft detailed and insightful maps that serve as the cornerstone for many geological endeavours. These maps reveal the surface features and provide a nuanced understanding of subsurface conditions. MapGeo professionals play an indispensable role in project planning, enabling stakeholders to navigate the complexities of the terrain with precision. Through their work, they unlock the mysteries of the Earth, empowering industries, researchers, and environmentalists alike to make informed decisions and confidently embark on ventures.

AquaGeo

In the realm of AquaGeo, specialists immerse themselves in the intricate dynamics of water systems. Through rigorous aquifer assessments, they unveil the secrets of underground water reservoirs, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of water availability. Water quality studies are conducted precisely, addressing contamination concerns and safeguarding this vital resource. AquaGeo professionals are not only stewards of water but also guardians against environmental degradation. Their work incorporates a profound awareness of natural hazards, contributing to sustainable water resource management. In a world increasingly aware of the importance of water, AquaGeo stands as a beacon of expertise and environmental responsibility.

EcoGeology

At the forefront of environmental stewardship, EcoGeologists embrace the profound interconnectedness between human activities and the Earth's delicate ecosystems. These experts go beyond conventional geological studies, delving into pollution mitigation, waste management, and the complex interplay of natural hazards. Their work is rooted in a commitment to sustainable practices, seeking a harmonious coexistence between human development and environmental preservation. EcoGeologists are architects of resilience, navigating the challenges posed by climate change and anthropogenic impact. In EcoGeology, every study, every recommendation, and every action is a testament to the pursuit of a balanced and sustainable planet.

Resourceology

Resourceologists are modern-day prospectors equipped with hammers and lenses and a deep commitment to sustainable resource management. In the pursuit of identifying mineral deposits, conducting oil and gas exploration, and evaluating water resources, they navigate a landscape fraught with challenges and opportunities. Beyond the excitement of discovery lies a responsibility to understand and address natural hazards, ensuring that the extraction of valuable resources aligns with ecological preservation. Resourceology is a discipline that recognizes the Earth's abundance but also acknowledges the fragility of its ecosystems. It is a science that seeks to balance human needs and environmental conservation, laying the groundwork for a future where prosperity and sustainability coexist

Publication Description
Morphological and Morphodynamical Aspects Applied to the Zoning of Relative Hazard by Landslide (Scale 1:100,000). UIS. electronic resource Technical report associated with the internship carried out within Colombia's National Development Plan 2010-2014 framework. This report describes zoning's key morphological and morphodynamic aspects, mainly focusing on sheet 119 – Barrancabermeja in the Santander department.
Surface geological units and geomorphological subunits (Scale 1:25,000). electronic resource Technical report of the pilot project for mapping surface geological units and geomorphological subunits conducted in the northern region of the Bolivar department, as Phase I of the detailed-scale hazard zoning to be carried out in Colombia. The project was developed in a special agreement with the Colombian Geological Survey, which provided project guidance and technical supervision in collaboration with the Corporation of the Canal del Dique and the Colombian Caribbean Observatory.
